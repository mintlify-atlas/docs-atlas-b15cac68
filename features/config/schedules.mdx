---
title: 'Work schedules'
description: 'How to create and manage work schedules, assign them to employees, and understand their relationship to attendance and overtime.'
---

Work schedules define the expected check-in time, check-out time, working days, and tolerance window for a group of employees. The attendance system uses the assigned schedule to determine whether an employee is on time or late.

## Schedule fields

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Name | Descriptive name (e.g., `Horario Operativo Lima`) |
| Shift | `MAÑANA`, `TARDE`, or `NOCHE` (optional) |
| Schedule type | `REGULAR`, `FERIADO`, or `DOMINGO` |
| Work days | Days of the week the schedule applies to (numeric: 1 = Monday, 7 = Sunday) |
| Sede | Optional sede restriction |
| Business unit | Optional business unit restriction |
| Position | Optional position restriction |
| Area | Optional area restriction |
| Check-in time | Expected entry time (`HH:MM`) |
| Check-out time | Expected exit time (`HH:MM`) |
| Tolerance minutes | Grace period in minutes before marking an employee as late |
| Bonus start / end | Early-arrival bonus window (applicable to OPL business units only) |
| Is active | Whether the schedule is available for assignment |

## How schedules relate to attendance

When an attendance record is processed, the system compares the employee's actual check-in time against the schedule's `check_in_time` plus `tolerance_minutes`. If the employee clocks in after that window, `is_late` is set to `true` on the attendance record.

For overtime calculation, the system compares the actual check-out time against `check_out_time`. Only employees in qualifying positions generate overtime records:

<Note>
Overtime (horas extras) is calculated only for employees with the following positions: **AUXILIAR DE ALMACÉN**, **MONTACARGUISTA**, **CONFERENTE**, and **COMPAGINADOR**. All other positions do not generate overtime regardless of check-out time.
</Note>

## The Schedules management page

Navigate to **Configuración → Gestión de Horarios**. The page has three tabs:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Horarios">
    Lists all schedules in a table. You can filter by sede, business unit, or search by name, position, or area. Admins and Jefes de Gente can create, edit, and delete schedules from this tab.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Asignaciones">
    Shows current active assignments (where `valid_to` is null) and allows assigning a schedule to one or more employees.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Horas Extras">
    Shows attendance records for qualifying positions within a date range, with calculated overtime minutes and a summary of total overtime hours and affected employees.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Creating a schedule

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Nuevo Horario">
    Available only to users with admin access or the Jefe de Gente position.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the required fields">
    At minimum, provide a name, check-in time, and check-out time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure optional restrictions">
    Set sede, business unit, position, and area fields to associate the schedule with a specific organizational scope. Leave them blank for a global schedule.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set work days">
    Select which days of the week this schedule applies to. The default is Monday through Saturday.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Guardar**. The new schedule appears in the table immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Editing a schedule

Click the pencil icon on any schedule row. The same modal opens pre-filled with the existing values. Save to apply changes.

## Deleting a schedule

Click the trash icon on the schedule row. A confirmation dialog appears before deletion.

<Warning>
Deleting a schedule does not automatically remove existing employee assignments. Assigned employees will show "sin horario" in overtime and attendance reports until a new schedule is assigned.
</Warning>

## Assigning a schedule to employees

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the Asignaciones tab">
    Switch to the **Asignaciones** tab on the Schedules management page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter and select employees">
    Use the search field, sede, and business unit filters to find employees. Check the boxes next to the employees you want to assign.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a schedule">
    Choose the schedule from the **Horario a asignar** dropdown. Only active schedules are listed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add optional notes">
    Enter any notes in the Observaciones field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Asignar Horario">
    The assignment is saved. Existing assignments for overlapping work days are automatically closed (their `valid_to` is set to today's date).
  </Step>
</Steps>

### How assignment replacement works

When a new schedule is assigned:

- **Regular schedules** close any existing assignment that shares at least one work day with the new schedule.
- **Special schedules** (`FERIADO` or `DOMINGO`) only close previous assignments of the same type. Regular schedules are not affected.
- Special schedule assignments are valid only for the `target_date` of the schedule (`valid_from = valid_to = target_date`).

## Removing an assignment

On the Asignaciones tab, click the **X** button on an assignment row. This sets `valid_to` to today's date on the `employee_schedule_assignments` record.
